Gherkin Grades & Sizes: A Buyer's Guide

March 24, 2026  ·  6 min read  ·  By AthiTerra Team

If you're sourcing gherkins for food manufacturing, one of the first specifications you'll encounter is the size grade. Gherkin sizing directly affects the texture, appearance, and suitability of the product for your specific application — whether you're packing retail jars, supplying burger slices to a fast-food chain, or producing relish at scale.

How Gherkins Are Graded

Gherkins are graded primarily by length (in centimetres) and by fruit count per kilogram. These two measures work together: smaller gherkins have a higher count per kilogram, while larger gherkins have fewer pieces per kilo. The industry uses standardised size ranges recognised across international trade.

Additionally, gherkins are assessed for uniformity, colour consistency, absence of defects (blemishes, curvature, soft spots), and firmness. A premium grade gherkin will be straight, firm, uniformly green, and free of mechanical damage.

The count-per-kilogram specification is critical for food manufacturers. It determines how many uniform pieces you get per unit weight, which directly affects portioning, cost per serving, and consistency across production batches.

Matching Size to Application

Cornichon grades (1–6 cm) are the premium segment, prized in French cuisine and upscale food retail. These tiny, crunchy gherkins command higher prices and are typically packed whole in glass jars with dill, mustard seed, or tarragon. Ideal for charcuterie boards, gourmet sandwich shops, and premium private-label brands.

Medium grades (6–9 cm) are the workhorse of the industry — the most commonly traded size globally. Suitable for whole-pack retail jars, slicing into hamburger chips for QSR chains, and dicing for relish or tartar sauce production.

Large grades (9–12 cm and above) are primarily used for slicing and dicing operations. A larger gherkin yields more uniform slices per piece, making it more efficient for food service applications where the gherkin won't be presented whole.

Preservation Medium Matters Too

Size is only half the specification. Gherkins are preserved in one of three media — acetic acid (standard for bulk industrial supply), natural vinegar (preferred for retail ready-to-eat products), or brine (mildest profile, ideal for custom seasoning). Each affects flavour, shelf life, and end-use suitability.

How to Specify Your Requirements

When requesting a quote from a gherkin supplier, communicate these key specs: size grade (in cm range), count per kilogram tolerance, preservation medium, packing format (drums, pails, jars), and required certifications (HACCP, BRC, Kosher, Organic, etc.).

A clear specification avoids back-and-forth and helps your supplier provide an accurate quote and lead time. If you're unsure which grade suits your application, a good supplier will send samples across multiple sizes for evaluation.
